Environment

Coelophysis lived during the late Triassic period (approximately 210 million years ago) in southwest North America. Hundreds of Coelophysis fossils have been found in the Ghost Ranch quarry of New Mexico, which is why it is the state fossil of New Mexico. The fossils found in the Ghost ranch quarry are thought to have died due to flash floods resulting from periodic monsoon rains, this is why such a high concentration of Coelophysis fossils have been found in that area. Coelophysis was one of the first dinosaurs. They lived before the golden age of dinosaurs in dry, desert-like areas with a savanna type climate (similar to Kenya).

Diet

Coelophysis was a carnivorous dinosaur that ate vertebrates. Coelophysis translates to "hollow form" due to its hollow vertebrae which made Coelophysis a swift and agile predator. Coelophysis also had sharp recurved and serrated teeth, which were effective in cutting through flesh. Coelophysis was suspected to have hunter smaller, quick-moving animals in packs.

Once mistaken to be cannibalistic, it was later revealed that it was actually fossilized crurotarsan reptiles that were in Coelophysis' fossilized abdomen, as opposed to its offspring.
